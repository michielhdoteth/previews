The Reef

Cozumel's Underwater Cathedral

Cozumel's reef system is a living cathedral. Spires of ancient coral rise from the deep, carved by centuries of gentle current into swim-throughs, overhangs, and walls that drop into ink-blue infinity. This is not a reef you visit. This is a reef you descend into.

Palancar Reef stretches for miles along the island's southern coast, its massive formations home to eagle rays, nurse sharks, and schooling grunts that darken the water above. Columbia Reef offers coral gardens that bloom with purple sea fans and orange elephant ear sponges. Paradise Reef, a shallow sanctuary of scattered coral heads, is where divers take their first breaths underwater and return as explorers.

Every dive is a drift along the second largest barrier reef system in the world. Visibility averages 80 feet. Water temperature sits at a perfect 78-82 F year-round. And with over 20 named dive sites within a 20-minute boat ride, you spend your time under the water, not on it.

Explore the Reefs

Signature Dive Sites

50-85 ft Advanced

Palancar Reef

Cozumel's most iconic reef system. Dramatic wall dives with massive coral formations, swim-through caverns, and deep drop-offs where eagle rays and nurse sharks patrol the edge. Advanced divers push to 85 feet along the coral spires.

40-80 ft Intermediate

Columbia Reef

A stunning drift dive through coral gardens and sand channels. Eagle rays soar overhead, turtles rest on the ledges, and the current carries you effortlessly past purple sea fans and massive barrel sponges.

30-55 ft Beginner

Paradise Reef

Perfect for new divers and families. Shallow coral heads teeming with tropical fish, starfish, and colorful reef life. A gentle introduction to Cozumel's underwater world with excellent visibility and minimal current.

40-65 ft Intermediate

Tormentos Reef

A marine life paradise where southern stingrays, green morays, and lobsters hide among the coral. The reef's gentle slope and steady current make it a favorite for underwater photographers and macro enthusiasts.
